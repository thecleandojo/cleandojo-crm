Who You Are

The story behind The Clean Dojo — your unfair advantage

Origin Story
You enrolled your three boys in BJJ to build discipline, humility, and confidence. You started training yourself. You fell in love with the sport and the community. You saw dirty mats and a gap nobody was filling — and you built the solution. This is not a cleaning company. This is a martial artist protecting the community he loves.
Who You Serve
Gym and dojo owners — mostly small business owners who are passionate about the sport but not always the best business operators. They care deeply about their students and their reputation. They've seen the white GI go brown at the knees. They know something is wrong but don't have a real solution.
Your Edge
You're not a cleaning company that cleans dojos. You're a martial artist who cleans dojos. You understand how mats are used, worn, and what's at stake. Owners feel that difference immediately.
"We don't just clean mats — we honor them."
"When the mats are clean, your team can roll with confidence."

Protective

Respectful

Authoritative

Personal
You're a dad. You train. You volunteered at the gym before you started charging. That authenticity is your most powerful differentiator. Use this on Instagram stories, reels, and anywhere you show your face. Father Martial artist Community member

Brand Identity

🥋
You are an insider, not a vendor
You trained BJJ. Your sons train BJJ. You've been on those mats. You're not selling cleaning to the martial arts community — you're protecting it from the inside. Every message should feel like it's coming from a training partner, not a salesperson.
👨‍👦
The father-founder story is your superpower
You enrolled your three boys. You put on the gi to be close to them. You saw the problem and built the solution. That story creates instant trust with gym owners who are also parents, and with parents who train. Use it often.
👊
Speak the language
Use the vocabulary: gi, roll, mat, dojo, academy, black belt, guard, tap. Never say "facility" when you can say "academy." Never say "clients" when you can say "students." You know this world. Let it show.
🔬
Lead with the invisible threat, close with confidence
Gym owners see dirty mats. What scares them is staph, ringworm, MRSA, a parent pulling their kid, a reputation hit. Open messages by naming what they already see (brown GIs, visible grime). Close by giving them the solution and confidence to fix it.
⚡
Your tagline is your anchor
"When the mats are clean, your team can roll with confidence." Use it or variations of it to close DMs, emails, captions, and proposals. It's simple, true, and speaks directly to what gym owners care about.

Proven Hooks

The Visual Hook
"I noticed something at your gym — the white GIs your students are wearing are going brown at the knees and back. That's not just dirt. That's bacteria that no regular mop is going to touch."
Use for: Cold DM openers · Works because it names something they've already seen
The Parent Hook
"I started The Clean Dojo because I put my three sons on the mats and realized most gyms don't have a real cleaning system — just a mop and hope. I wasn't okay with that as a parent. And I didn't think you would be either."
Use for: Instagram captions · Email intros · Talking to gyms with kids programs
The Reputation Hook
"One staph outbreak doesn't just sideline a student. It can end a gym's reputation. I've seen it. The Clean Dojo exists so you never have to explain to a parent why their kid got ringworm at your academy."
Use for: Competition gyms · High-reputation academies · Follow-up messages
The Respect Hook
"In jiu-jitsu, we bow before we step on the mat. We treat the mat with respect. We built The Clean Dojo because that respect should extend to how clean that mat is when your students step on it."
Use for: Instagram captions · Brand storytelling · Website copy

Do's & Don'ts

✓ Always Do

  • Use martial arts vocabulary naturally
  • Reference your personal story when relevant
  • Name the specific health risk (staph, ringworm, MRSA)
  • Speak to both the gym owner AND the parent
  • Close with confidence, not desperation
  • Keep DMs short — 3–5 sentences max
  • Use "academy" not "facility"
  • Use "students" not "clients" when talking about their people
  • Mention commercial-grade equipment

✗ Never Do

  • Sound like a generic cleaning company
  • Lead with price in the first message
  • Use corporate language ("solutions," "leverage," "synergy")
  • Send walls of text in a DM
  • Beg or follow up more than 3 times
  • Pretend you clean everything — you specialize
  • Forget the emotional angle (health, reputation, trust)
  • Use "hi there" or "dear sir/madam" openers

Your 20k Growth Roadmap — 3 Phases
1
Phase 1 — Own the Niche (7k → 12k)
Months 1–2 · Double down on what's working
🎬
Post every before/after you have — back to back
Your before/after content already works. Post every clean job this month as a Reel. Same format, different gym. Algorithm rewards consistency in format. Aim for 3 per week minimum.
🎵
Add trending BJJ audio to your Reels
Go to Instagram Reels, find trending audio in the martial arts/fitness space, and use it on your before/after videos. Same footage, trending sound = 3-5x more reach. CapCut makes this easy.
💬
Spend 20 minutes a day commenting on BJJ accounts
Go to the top 20 BJJ gym pages and influencers. Leave genuine, thoughtful comments — not "great content!" but real observations. People click your profile. This is free follower growth.
🦠
Post one "scary" education Reel per week
Topic ideas: "What actually lives on unwashed BJJ mats," "Why your students keep getting ringworm," "The one thing a mop can't clean." Fear-based education gets shared aggressively in parent groups and gym owner communities.
2
Phase 2 — Build Authority (12k → 17k)
Months 3–4 · Become the go-to voice in the niche
🤝
Collaborate with BJJ gym owners and influencers
Reach out to 5-10 gym owners with 10k+ followers. Offer to clean their gym for free or reduced rate in exchange for a collab Reel. One share from a 50k account can add 2,000+ followers in a day. This is your fastest growth lever.
📚
Launch a carousel series — "The Clean Mat Files"
Weekly carousel series covering BJJ mat hygiene education. "Episode 1: Staph vs MRSA — what's the difference." "Episode 2: How often should a 1,000sqft mat space be cleaned." Carousels get saved and shared — the algorithm loves saves.
👨‍👦
Show your sons training — the personal story content
The martial arts community connects hard with family content. A Reel of you and your sons on the mat with a voiceover about why you started The Clean Dojo will outperform your before/after content. Film it once, use it forever.
🏆
Tag every gym you clean
When you post a before/after, tag the gym's Instagram account. When they reshare it to their audience — which most will, because it's free marketing for them too — their followers see you. Every tag is a potential 500-2,000 new followers.
3
Phase 3 — Scale & Monetize (17k → 20k+)
Months 5–6 · Turn followers into revenue
🎓
Tease your course to your audience
At 15k+ followers you have a real audience for your course launch. Start teasing it 60 days before. "I'm building something for gym owners who want to handle their own mat cleaning professionally. More soon." Build anticipation before the launch.
📍
Launch city-specific content for licensee recruitment
"Who's the Clean Dojo of Dallas?" "Who's bringing professional mat cleaning to New York?" Content that plants the seed for your licensee program while also growing your national following. This is how you recruit and grow simultaneously.
📱
Launch TikTok and Facebook simultaneously
Repurpose every Reel to TikTok and Facebook Reels. Same content, three platforms. TikTok's algorithm is more aggressive for new accounts — you could gain 5,000 followers in a week on a single viral video. This is your 20k accelerator.
🔧
Machine sale content at scale
At 17k+ followers, a single "this is the machine we use" Reel with a link in bio will generate machine sales on autopilot. The audience trust is already built. One post can generate $3,000-$10,000 in machine sales in 48 hours.

Weekly Non-Negotiables — Do These Every Week No Matter What

🎬
3 Reels minimum
Monday, Thursday, Saturday. Reel days are sacred. Miss a posting day and the algorithm punishes you for 2 weeks.
💬
20 minutes of community engagement daily
Comment on BJJ accounts, reply to every DM within 2 hours, respond to every comment on your posts. The algorithm rewards engagement rate, not just follower count.
📖
One story per day
Stories keep you top of mind with existing followers. Behind the scenes of a job, a poll ("how often does your gym clean its mats?"), a quick tip. 30 seconds of effort.
🏷️
Tag every gym you visit
Every single one. Even if they don't reshare it immediately. Over time their followers see your content in the tags and follow you. Compound effect over months is massive.
